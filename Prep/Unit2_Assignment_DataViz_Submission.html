us_data = df[df['Country or Area'] == 'United States of America']

plt.plot(us_data['Year'], us_data['Value'], color='red')
plt.ylim([0, max(us_data['Value']) + 100000])
plt.ylabel('in kilotonne CO2 equivalent')
plt.title('Carbon dioxide (CO2) Emissions without Land Use, Land-Use Change and Forestry (LULUCF) of U.S.')
plt.show()

The chart above is a line chart. This one illustrate the development of the emision of CO2 in U.S. over time from 1990 to 2016.

Global Emission¶

In [6]:
sum_per_year = df.groupby('Year').aggregate(np.sum)
plt.plot(sum_per_year['Value'], color='red')
plt.ylim([0, max(sum_per_year['Value']) + 1000000])
plt.ylabel('in kilotonne CO2 equivalent')
plt.title('GLOBAL Carbon dioxide (CO2) Emissions without Land Use, Land-Use Change and Forestry (LULUCF)')
plt.show()

The chart above shows the world wide CO2 emission development over time (from 1990 to 2016). It is displayed as a line chart.
